How to Calculate Exact Water Requirement for Any Crop

April 24, 2026 Crop Guides

The problem: Most farmers irrigate based on a schedule (e.g., "every morning for 2 hours"). This either drowns the roots or leaves the plant thirsty. Both lead to yield loss.

The Science of Evapotranspiration

Plants don't just "drink" water; they lose it through their leaves (transpiration) and the soil loses it through heat (evaporation). Combined, this is called Evapotranspiration (ET). To calculate exact water needs, you need to know the Reference ET (ETo) for your area and the Crop Coefficient (Kc).

ETc = ETo × Kc

Breaking Down the Formula

  • ETo (Reference ET): How much water a standard grass field would lose today. You get this from weather stations or APIs.
  • Kc (Crop Coefficient): A value that changes based on the plant type and its growth stage. A seedling uses less water (low Kc) than a fruit-bearing plant (high Kc).

Technical Insight: Soil Moisture Deficit

You shouldn't just replace the ETc every day. Soil has a "holding capacity" like a battery. You only irrigate when the "charge" (moisture level) drops below a certain point. This is called the Management Allowed Depletion (MAD).

By combining ETc calculation with MAD logic, we can reduce water consumption by up to 30% while increasing crop quality.

Conclusion

Stop guessing. Start measuring. Using a simple Kc table and a local weather API, you can transform your irrigation from "art" to "engineering."
