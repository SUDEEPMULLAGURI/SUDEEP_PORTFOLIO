Drip vs Flood Irrigation: Which Is Better?

April 24, 2026 Low-Cost Solutions

The problem: Water is finite. Traditional flood irrigation wastes up to 60% of water through evaporation and deep percolation. Drip is the future, but it has baggage.

The Efficiency War

In flood irrigation, we saturate the entire field surface. This encourages weed growth and causes nutrient leaching. Drip irrigation, however, targets only the Root Zone. It keeps the surface dry (killing weeds) and saves water. No contest, right?

Where Drip Fails

Drip systems require filtration. If your water source comes from a silted river or an open well with algae, your emitters will clog in 24 hours. A drip system without a ₹20,000 sand filter is a disaster waiting to happen.

Technical Insight: Salt Management

Flood irrigation naturally "washes" salts away from the plant. Drip irrigation can actually concentrate salts at the edge of the wet bulb, potentially toxic to the roots. Proper drip systems must include a "flushing" cycle to manage soil salinity.

Conclusion

Drip is 90% efficient, Flood is 40%. The choice is clear, but only if you have the technical discipline to maintain the filters and clean the lines.
