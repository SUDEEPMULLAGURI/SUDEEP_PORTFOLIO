Tomato Water Requirement Per Acre (Complete Guide)

April 24, 2026 Crop Guides

The problem: Tomatoes are incredibly sensitive to water fluctuations. Too little and you get blossom end rot. Too much and the fruit cracks. Precision is the difference between profit and loss.

Here is an engineering-first breakdown of tomato water needs for a 1-acre field.

Phase 1: Initial Growth (0-30 Days)

During the nursery and transplant phase, water needs are minimal but critical for root establishment. You typically need 2,000 to 4,000 liters per acre daily depending on climate.

Phase 2: Flowering & Fruit Set (30-60 Days)

This is the danger zone. Water stress here causes flowers to drop. Demand increases to 6,000 to 10,000 liters per acre.

Critical Tech Tip: Maintain soil moisture at 70-80% field capacity. Fluctuations here will lead to uneven fruit growth and splitting.

Phase 3: Fruit Ripening (60+ Days)

Surprisingly, you should slightly decrease water as fruit matures to increase the TSS (Brix) level and flavor intensity. Use 5,000 to 7,000 liters.

Technical Insight: Drip Scheduling

Don't dump 8,000 liters at once. Use Pulse Irrigation—break it into three sessions throughout the day. This keeps the root zone consistently moist without leaching nutrients into the deep soil.

Conclusion

By measuring ETc and using pulse drip irrigation, a tomato farmer can expect up to a 25% increase in grade-A fruit yield.
